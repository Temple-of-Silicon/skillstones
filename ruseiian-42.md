### Romanization Key

Special romanizations:
- ny = /ɲ/
- ng = /ŋ/
- sh = /ʃ/
- ch = /tʃ/
- gh = /ɣ/
- kh = /x/

### Syllable Structure

**Formula:** (C)V(C)

(C)V(C) (simple syllables)

- Maximum onset: 1 consonant(s)
- Maximum coda: 1 consonant(s)

### Phonological Rules

**Nasal Assimilation:** /n/ assimilates to place of following stop
  Rule: `n → m / _[+labial], n → ŋ / _[+velar]`

**Vowel Harmony:** Vowels agree in height within a word
  Rule: `V → [αheight] / V[αheight]___`

**Palatalization:** Velars become palatals before front vowels
  Rule: `k → tʃ / _[+front], g → dʒ / _[+front]`



## §1.1 Prosody

### Summary

fixed stress, mora-timed rhythm

### Stress System

**Type:** Fixed penultimate stress

**Primary Stress:** Always on penultimate syllable
**Position:** penultimate
**Secondary Stress:** Alternating (every other syllable from primary)

### Rhythm Type

**Mora-timed (isochronous morae)**

- Equal time for each mora
- Long vowels = 2 morae
- Coda consonants may be moraic
- Very regular rhythm

*Similar to: Japanese, Finnish*

### Intonation Patterns

| Type | Contour | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| statement | level-low | The cat sat on the mat. ↘ |
| yes-no question | high-rising | Is the cat on the mat? ↗ |
| wh-question | high-falling | Where is the cat? ↘ |
| continuation | level | When I got home, → ... |
| focus | pitch-peak | THE CAT sat on the mat. |
