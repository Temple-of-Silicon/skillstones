## §1.1 Prosody

### Summary

fixed stress, mora-timed rhythm

### Stress System

**Type:** Fixed penultimate stress

**Primary Stress:** Always on penultimate syllable
**Position:** penultimate

#### Stress Effects

- **Vowel reduction:** Unstressed vowels reduce to schwa or centralize
- **Stressed vowel lengthening:** Stressed vowels are phonetically longer

### Rhythm Type

**Mora-timed (isochronous morae)**

- Equal time for each mora
- Long vowels = 2 morae
- Coda consonants may be moraic
- Very regular rhythm

*Similar to: Japanese, Finnish*

### Intonation Patterns

| Type | Contour | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| statement | falling | The cat sat on the mat. ↘ |
| yes-no question | rising | Is the cat on the mat? ↗ |
| wh-question | level | Where is the cat? ↘ |
| continuation | slight-rise | When I got home, → ... |
| focus | pitch-peak | THE CAT sat on the mat. |
