## §1.1 Prosody

### Summary

fixed stress, stress-timed rhythm

### Stress System

**Type:** Fixed initial stress

**Primary Stress:** Always on initial syllable
**Position:** initial
**Secondary Stress:** Alternating (every other syllable from primary)

#### Stress Effects

- **Stressed vowel lengthening:** Stressed vowels are phonetically longer
- **Pitch accent:** Stressed syllables have higher pitch

### Rhythm Type

**Stress-timed (isochronous stresses)**

- Equal time between stressed syllables
- Unstressed syllables compressed
- Variable syllable length
- Strong vowel reduction

*Similar to: English, German, Russian*

### Intonation Patterns

| Type | Contour | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| statement | falling | The cat sat on the mat. ↘ |
| yes-no question | high-rising | Is the cat on the mat? ↗ |
| wh-question | falling | Where is the cat? ↘ |
| continuation | slight-rise | When I got home, → ... |
| focus | lengthening | THE CAT sat on the mat. |

## §3 Syntax

### Basic Word Order

**SVO** - Subject-Verb-Object (e.g., "The cat saw the mouse")

### Phrase Structure

**Noun Phrases:**
- Adjective position: after noun
- Genitive position: before noun
- Determiner position: typically before noun

**Example NP structure:** (DET) NOUN (ADJ)

### Questions

**Yes/No Questions:** Add question particle **khoch** at end of sentence.

**Content Questions:** Question word appears in situ (in the position of the questioned element).

### Negation

Negation via suffix

### Subordination

Relative clauses: precede the head noun.
Complement clauses: typically follow the main verb.

**Base:** vigesimal (20)
**Representation:** dedicated numerals
**Zero:** Has zero symbol

### Romanization Scheme

- One-to-one phoneme-to-letter mapping where possible
